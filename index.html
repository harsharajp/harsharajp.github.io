Hi there!


I am Harsharaj Parmar and I've designed this webspace to give the details of my evolution as a researcher. Currently, I am a graduate student at Purdue University working under Prof. David M. Warsinger in his water lab. Our team develops novel thermal desalination strategies for efficient and high recovery treatment of high salinity feed waters.

My research interests broadly span across the fields of Fluid Mechanics and Thermodynamics. In particular, I am inclined towards numerical analysis of heat transfer, fluid flows (Computational Fluid Dynamics) and thermodynamic modelling of practical systems.

I've been previously affiliated with the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati where I completed my undergraduate studies and Hanyang University, South Korea as an undergraduate researcher. At Hanyang University, I worked under Prof. Yoomin Ahn at the MEMS lab designing membraneless microfluidic fuel cells. More information on my coursework, independant projects and prior research can be found in the Academics and Research sections.

Apart from academics, I have been associated with IIT Guwahati's FSAE club and taken part in various institute-level technical events. I am an avid Formula 1 enthusiast who loves karting, working out and running.

Feel free to Contact me in case of any clarifications or discussions!
